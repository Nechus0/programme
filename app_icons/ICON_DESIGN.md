# Icon Philosophy — "Clinical Clarity"

A favicon is read at 16 pixels before it is ever read at 512. Its job is not to
illustrate everything the product does, but to be *unmistakable* at a glance in a
crowded tab bar. The previous mark failed because it tried to say "cross + syringe +
circle" all at once; at 16px that collapses into a grey smudge.

**One shape, maximum contrast.** The new mark keeps a single dominant form — the open
ring (the Charité "C" motif already present in the header wordmark) rendered as a thick
white stroke on a near-black squircle. Solid figure, solid ground, nothing thin enough
to disappear when downsampled.

**Brand echo, not brand copy.** The open ring quietly quotes the institutional wordmark
without reproducing the protected logotype — recognisable to anyone who knows Charité,
a clean abstract ring to everyone else.

**Monochrome discipline.** Charité's system is black, grey and white. The icon holds to
that restraint: no gradients, no colour, no bevels — the kind of decision that reads as
considered rather than decorative.

**Built to scale.** Rendered at 4096px and downsampled with anti-aliasing to every
required size, so the 16px tab icon and the 1024px store icon are the same idea executed
with the same precision — meticulous at every resolution.
